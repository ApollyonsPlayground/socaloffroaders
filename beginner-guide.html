Understanding Your 4x4

🚗

4x4 vs 2WD vs AWD

4x4 (4WD): Power to all 4 wheels, selectable. Best for off-roading. Can be part-time or full-time.

AWD: Always powers all wheels, automatic. Good for light off-road and slippery conditions.

2WD: Power to 2 wheels only. Not suitable for most off-road trails.

⚙️

4-High vs 4-Low

4-High (4H): Use for loose surfaces like dirt, gravel, sand. Higher speed capability.

4-Low (4L): Maximum torque for crawling, steep climbs, rock obstacles. Low speed only.

2WD: Normal driving on pavement. Never use 4WD on dry pavement!

🔒

What Are Lockers?

Open Differential: Wheels spin independently. If one loses traction, it spins freely.

Locker: Locks both wheels on an axle together. Both spin at same speed regardless of traction.

When you need them: Rock crawling, extreme trails, deep ruts. Not needed for beginner trails.

Buying Your First 4x4

🌱 Stock Truck

$15k-40k

  • ✅ Perfect for beginner trails
  • ✅ Comfortable daily driver
  • ✅ Learn skills before modifying
  • ✅ Warranty coverage

Examples: Toyota Tacoma, Jeep Wrangler Sport, Ford Bronco Sport, 4Runner SR5

Recommended

🔧 Mild Build

$25k-50k+

  • ✅ Handles moderate trails
  • ✅ Better clearance & tires
  • ✅ Recovery points
  • ✅ Room to grow

2-3" lift, 33" tires, sliders, winch bumper

🏔️ Built Rig

$40k-80k+

  • ✅ Extreme trail capable
  • ✅ Lockers, big tires, armor
  • ✅ Go anywhere confidence
  • ⚠️ Overkill for beginners

4-6" lift, 35-37" tires, front/rear lockers, full armor

What to Look For

✓
4WD System

Part-time 4WD with 4-High and 4-Low

✓
Ground Clearance

8+ inches minimum for trails

✓
Tires

All-terrain or mud-terrain, not highway

✓
Recovery Points

Front and rear tow hooks or shackle mounts

✓
Low Range

2.5:1 or lower crawl ratio for control

✓
Skid Plates

Protect engine, transmission, transfer case

Trail Etiquette

🛤️

Stay on Designated Trails

Creating new trails damages the environment and gets areas closed. Stay on marked routes.

🗑️

Pack It Out

Everything you bring in, bring out. Including toilet paper and food scraps. Leave it cleaner than you found it.

🤝

Yield Uphill

Vehicles going uphill have right of way. It's harder to restart on a hill than to stop downhill.

💡

Dim Your Lights

When meeting oncoming traffic, dim your lights. Don't blind other drivers on tight trails.

🔊

Keep Noise Down

Respect others enjoying the outdoors. Loud music and excessive revving disturb wildlife and other users.

🆘

Help Others

The off-roading community looks out for each other. Stop and help when you can. Karma is real on the trails.
